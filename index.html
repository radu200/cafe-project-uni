Cafe In Paris

Cafe De La Paix

Ever since their inauguration on May 5th, 1862, the Hotel de la Paix (which then became the InterContinental Paris le Grand Hotel) and the Café de la Paix are genuine institutions enjoying worldwide fame. The Café de la Paix is a restaurant bar exuding Second Empire elegance and proudly displaying its listed frescoes and sumptuous gilding. Spirited gastronomy is the hallmark of this outstanding venue, which also serves a selection of fast fare all day long in the indoor terrace. When fair weather returns, it unfolds its renowned summer terrace facing the flamboyant Opéra-Garnier.

Address: 5 Place de l'Opéra, 75009 Paris, France
Nearest Underground: Opéra

Les Deux Magots

The name of the café “Les Deux Magots” (i.e. “two Chinese figurines”) comes from a novelty shop that once occupied the same premises. Founded in 1812 at 23 Rue de Buci, it was transferred to Place St-Germain-des-Prés to expand in 1873. The two statues that adorn the café now stand as witnesses to that era...

Address: 6 Place Saint-Germain des Prés, 75006 Paris, France
Nearest Underground: Saint-Germain-des-Prés

Café Verlet

Since 1880 Verlet has been a purveyor of teas and coffees, spices and candied fruit. At the same time, the house at 256 rue Saint-Honoré opened an exquisite gourmet emporium, one of the rare Parisian shops to offer top quality home roasted coffee brewed directly on the footpath in front of the shop. Every morning, a high temperature metal drum was hand cranked to evenly roast and brew pale green beans from the Americas, the West Indies and Africa, until the perfect roast « à la française » was achieved. The renowned colour « Robe de Moine » became a Verlet speciality. The delicious fragrance of coffee filled the neighbourhood and every connoisseur and reputable home shopped at Verlet.

Address: 256 Rue Saint-Honoré, 75001 Paris, France
Nearest Underground: Pyramides
